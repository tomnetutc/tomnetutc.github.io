Tracking Wellbeing in Society

An Interactive Wellbeing Assessment Tool by TOMNET

Measurement of wellbeing is critical for addressing social disparities and promoting equitable and sustainable living. Here we present two wellbeing metrics that are designed to assess how wellbeing has changed over time and place for different population subgroups.

W-BEAT

W-BEATs is developed based on the wellbeing modules available in the 2010, 2012, and 2013 editions of the American Time Use Survey (ATUS). It translates time use and activity participation patterns of individuals into wellbeing scores while controlling for their socio-demographic and economic attributes. The estimated scores reflects overall wellbeing of individuals. When these scores are aggregated from person level, they can be used to compare the well-being of one group to that of other groups in the population. More details about the methodology can be found here. The developed W-BEAT model was applied to all editions of American Time Use Survey since 2003.
